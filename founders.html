Founders

Note: all people on this page (with the exception of Jack Sanders) are entirely fictitious, and their images were created using artbreeder

Jack Sanders founded Lambton Entertainment in 2021, with a clear vision to provide the best games for young children, to help them learn about the world and creatures in it. He was inspired to do this after a long trip to the baobab trees of Madagascar, in which he would witness a small child refer to a bizarre-nosed chameleon as a 'gecko'. This heinous misidentification set him on a course to change the lives of tens of young kids around the globe. Now, kids will be able to easily tell their mountain chickens from their appenzell pointed hood hens, and most importantly, have fun while doing so!

Julie Parkins met Jack Sanders on that fateful Madagascar odyssey, where he shared with her the tale of the harrowing events of the day before. With just a Greggs Sausage Roll, two Lucozades, and a pet dog, they set off on a journey greater than the Hobbit. Through many sleepless siestas, they built an empire of childrens' games, founded on the basis that no critically endangered species should ever be misidentified again.

Terrence Walters-Toasters is an old friend of Jack Sanders,who he met at the world-renowned ToasterCon in 2007, in London - although, Terrence doesn't actually like toasters, he was just there because he thought it was a family gathering. They bonded over a mutual love of identifying various objects/animals, and so he was recruited into Lambton Entertainment as toastmaster-in-chief in December of 2021, due to his excellent public speaking skills and incredibly apt surname.
